# Monivo — Landing Page (`monivo.lt`)

The landing page exists to sell **emotional clarity**, not features.

A visitor should leave knowing one thing: *"This will finally tell me how much is mine."*

---

## 1. Purpose

- Convert Lithuanian self-employed beauty professionals into trial users of `app.monivo.lt`.
- Establish Monivo as **premium, calm, and trustworthy** within 3 seconds of landing.
- Differentiate clearly from accounting software, bank apps, and generic budgeting tools.

It is **not** a feature catalog. It is **not** a comparison table. It is **not** an SEO content farm.

---

## 2. Conversion Goals

Primary CTA: **"Pradėti nemokamai"** → opens the app onboarding.

Secondary action: **"Sužinoti daugiau"** → scrolls to the explainer section.

Single conversion event. No newsletter signup. No lead magnet. No popup.

---

## 3. Emotional Positioning

The visitor is tired. She has been on her feet for nine hours. She has cash in her wallet, a Revolut notification, and no idea how much of any of it is actually hers after taxes.

Monivo's landing page must feel like **a deep breath**.

Tone:
- **Warm**, not corporate.
- **Confident**, not loud.
- **Quiet**, not empty.
- **Lithuanian**, not translated.

Avoid:
- Hustle culture language ("grow your business", "scale your income").
- Productivity bro tropes.
- "AI-powered" anything.
- Stock photos of generic businesspeople.

---

## 4. Section Structure

The landing page is a **single, focused scroll**. No mega-menu, no footer maze.

1. **Hero**
2. **The problem (one line)**
3. **The promise (the spendable number)**
4. **How it works (3 steps)**
5. **Who it's for (beauty pros)**
6. **What Monivo is *not***
7. **Closing CTA**
8. **Minimal footer**

That's it. Eight blocks. No more.

---

## 5. Hero Direction

- Full-bleed, mobile-first.
- Background: `cream` with a single soft, blurred shape behind the headline (no photography).
- Headline: **"Pagaliau aišku kiek lieka."**
- Subhead (one sentence): *"Monivo padeda grožio specialistams kasdien matyti, kiek pinigų iš tiesų jų — be apskaitos, be streso."*
- Primary CTA button: *Pradėti nemokamai*.
- Secondary text link: *Kaip tai veikia →*
- Visual anchor: a **single device mockup** of the dashboard, showing the spendable hero card. No carousel. No floating screenshots.

Above the fold on a 375px viewport must contain: headline, subhead, primary CTA, and the top of the mockup.

---

## 6. Messaging Tone

Lithuanian-first, written like a calm peer — not a bank, not a startup pitch.

Examples (use as direction, not literal copy):

- *"Įvedi pajamas. Monivo automatiškai atideda mokesčius. Tu matai tik tai, ką gali išleisti."*
- *"Be lentelių. Be apskaitos terminų. Be nerimo."*
- *"Sukurta nagų, blakstienų, kosmetologijos ir kirpyklų meistrams."*

Rules:
- Short sentences. Often one line.
- No exclamation marks.
- No emojis.
- No buzzwords ("revoliucinis", "AI", "next-gen").
- Numbers are concrete: "10 sekundžių", not "fast".

---

## 7. CTA Strategy

- **One** primary CTA repeated 3 times: hero, after "how it works", final section.
- Identical label every time: *Pradėti nemokamai*.
- Primary CTA never opens a modal. It always navigates to `app.monivo.lt`.
- No urgency tactics (countdowns, "only X spots").
- No social-proof inflation (no fake counters).

---

## 8. Visual Hierarchy

Per section:
1. **One headline.**
2. **One supporting line.**
3. **One visual anchor** (mockup, illustration, or a stylized number).
4. **One action** (only on hero and closing sections).

Whitespace is the primary design tool. Sections breathe — at least 80px vertical separation on mobile, 120px on desktop.

Typography hierarchy: display headline → supporting body → small caption. Nothing else.

---

## 9. Screenshot Strategy

- **One high-quality mockup** in the hero (the dashboard with the spendable number).
- **One detail mockup** in "How it works" (the quick-add sheet).
- **One activity mockup** in "Who it's for".
- That is the entire visual inventory.

Rules:
- Mockups are real product UI, not Figma frames with placeholder text.
- All numbers shown are realistic (e.g., `€1 247`), in EUR, with LT formatting.
- All copy in mockups is in Lithuanian.
- Mockups sit on `cream` background with a soft shadow — no device frames, no laptop bezels.
- No tilted 3D mockups. Flat, straight, calm.

---

## 10. "What Monivo is not" Section

This section is unusual but important. It directly addresses the user's mental model.

Example direction:

> **Monivo nėra**
> – buhalterinė programa
> – banko aplikacija
> – rezervacijų sistema
>
> **Monivo yra** vienas skaičius, kuriam gali patikėti.

This builds trust by saying out loud what the user is silently worried about.

---

## 11. Footer

Minimal. One row on desktop, stacked on mobile.

- Logo
- `Privatumas` · `Sąlygos` · `Kontaktai`
- `© Monivo`

No newsletter. No social icons unless real accounts exist. No "Made with ❤".

---

## 12. SEO Basics

- One `<h1>` per page (the hero headline).
- Meta title: *Monivo — Pagaliau aišku kiek lieka*
- Meta description: one calm sentence, ≤ 155 chars.
- Open Graph image: the hero mockup on cream background.
- `lang="lt"`.
- Structured data for `SoftwareApplication` (locale `lt-LT`).

---

## 13. What the Landing Page Must Never Become

- A feature list with checkmarks.
- A comparison table with competitors.
- A pricing matrix.
- A testimonials wall full of fake quotes.
- A blog hub.
- A "trusted by" logo bar with logos we don't have permission to use.
- A page that requires JavaScript to read.
