# Monivo — Product

## 1. Vision

Monivo gives Lithuanian self-employed beauty professionals a calm, instant answer to the only money question that actually matters during the workday:

> *"How much of this is really mine?"*

It replaces the spreadsheet, the mental math, and the low-grade financial anxiety that follows every cash or transfer payment with a single, reassuring number.

The product is built around one feeling: **clarity**.

---

## 2. Target Audience

Primary:
- Nail artists (manikiūrininkės)
- Lash artists (blakstienų meistrės)
- Cosmetologists (kosmetologės)
- Hairdressers (kirpėjai/-os)
- Independent beauty freelancers working under *individuali veikla* or *verslo liudijimas*

Shared characteristics:
- Works alone or in a small rented studio.
- Gets paid in a mix of cash, bank transfers, and Revolut.
- Has irregular daily income.
- Is not an accountant and does not want to become one.
- Uses an iPhone or Android primarily, almost never a desktop.
- Speaks Lithuanian as their first language.

Secondary (future): other Lithuanian solo service providers — tattoo artists, massage therapists, personal trainers.

---

## 3. User Pain Points

1. **"I don't know how much I actually earned this month."** Income arrives in fragments across cash, IBAN, and Revolut.
2. **"I spent the tax money."** No mental separation between gross income and what's owed to VMI/Sodra.
3. **"Excel is too much."** Existing tools are either accounting software (too heavy) or generic budgeting apps (not built for self-employment).
4. **"I avoid looking at my finances."** The current tools cause anxiety, not relief.
5. **"My accountant handles the paperwork, but I still don't *feel* in control."** Compliance is solved; clarity is not.

---

## 4. Emotional Goals

The product must make the user feel:

- **Calm** — opening the app should lower the heart rate, not raise it.
- **In control** — they always know the spendable number.
- **Respected** — the design treats them as a professional, not a small business "user."
- **Unjudged** — no streaks, no shaming, no gamification of money.
- **Light** — using Monivo should feel like 10 seconds, not a chore.

## 5. Positioning

| | Monivo | Accounting app (Apskaita5, i.MAS) | Budgeting app (YNAB, generic) | Bank app |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Audience | LT beauty solo pros | Accountants & SMBs | General consumers | All bank customers |
| Purpose | Daily clarity | Compliance | Personal budgeting | Transactions |
| Tone | Calm, premium, simple | Technical, formal | Generic, gamified | Transactional |
| Time to value | <10 seconds | Days | Hours | Minutes |

Monivo sits in the **clarity** category, which today is empty for this audience.

---

## 6. What Monivo Does

- Quick income entry (amount, optional category, optional note).
- Quick expense entry (amount, optional category).
- Automatic tax reserve (configurable %, defaults aligned with common LT self-employment scenarios).
- A single **Spendable** number on the home screen.
- Chronological activity history.
- Simple monthly insights: total income, total expenses, reserved for taxes, spendable.

That is the MVP. Nothing more.

---

## 7. What Monivo Does NOT Do

- Does **not** generate invoices.
- Does **not** file taxes or talk to VMI/Sodra.
- Does **not** replace an accountant.
- Does **not** connect to bank accounts (no Open Banking in MVP).
- Does **not** manage clients or bookings.
- Does **not** do payroll, inventory, or multi-user features.
- Does **not** do crypto, investments, or savings goals.
- Does **not** export complex reports.

If a feature request lands in any of the above categories, the default answer is **no**.

---

## 8. MVP Scope

### Must have
1. **Income tracking** — one-tap quick add with amount + optional category.
2. **Expense tracking** — same flow as income.
3. **Tax reserve** — user sets a percentage once during onboarding; every income entry contributes automatically.
4. **Spendable amount** — surfaced on the home screen as the hero number.
5. **Activity history** — chronological list, filterable by month.
6. **Simple insights** — one monthly summary screen.
7. **PWA install flow** — add-to-home-screen on iOS and Android.
8. **Lithuanian-first UI.**

### Explicitly out of MVP
- Multi-currency
- Recurring entries
- Receipt scanning
- Categories beyond a small fixed set
- Charts beyond one summary
- Sharing or accountant export
- Notifications

---

## 9. Future Ideas (post-MVP, not committed)

- Optional accountant export (CSV/PDF).
- Recurring expenses (rent, supplies).
- Smart category suggestions.
- Yearly tax reserve view.
- Light dark mode.
- Apple Wallet–style widget.
- English UI for non-LT solo beauty pros.

These are explicitly **not** to be built or designed for during MVP.

---

## 10. The North Star

Every decision is checked against one question:

> *Does this make it easier for a tired nail artist at 8pm to know how much of today's money is hers?*

If yes — consider it.
If no — cut it.
